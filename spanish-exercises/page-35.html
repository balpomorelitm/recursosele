ASKING IN SPANISH

Yes / No Questions

In Spanish, the same verb forms used in statements are used in questions. A statement can be made into a question simply by a change in intonation (the voice tone) or, in writing, by adding question marks:

Hablas español. > You speak Spanish.

¿Hablas español? > Do you speak Spanish?

Note that in English an auxiliary verb is used (do) to form questions, but this is not the case in Spanish.

Interrogatives (or questions words)

Just like in English, interrogatives are typically placed at or very near the beginning of a sentence. Some of them are used like in English (when: cuándo, where: dónde); some of them exist in singular and plural forms (who: quién/es), and in the case of how much / many (cuánto/a/os/as) there are singular, plural, masculine and feminine forms that should match the noun they stand for. However, qué, cuál/es and cómo do not always correspond to what, which and how, but they depend on what we use them for. Have a look at this table:

Remember:

  • Question words always have an accent.
  • Don't forget to add the opening upside-down question mark (¿) in Spanish questions.

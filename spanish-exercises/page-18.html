VERBS

As you saw in unit 1, there are:

› Regular verbs: the spelling of the root is always the same:

VIVIR > vivo, vives, vive, vivimos, vivís, viven.

› Irregular verbs: the spelling of the root is not always the same:

SER > soy, eres, es, somos, sois, son.

An example of an irregular verb in English is to be (ser), but in Spanish there are more irregular verbs. Some of them change completely, like ser, but some others change for the first person singular like hacer (hago, haces...) or some have a small change in spelling, like tener (tengo, tienes, tiene...).
